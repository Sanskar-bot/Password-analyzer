    Why targeted attacks are different
    Standard dictionary attacks use wordlists of millions of common passwords. They work against generic choices like "password123" or "qwerty".
    Personalized attacks are different. An attacker who knows your name, birthday, or pet's name will generate combinations like Bruno2004, Sanskar@123, or Phougat#07 — passwords that pass generic strength checks but fail against a targeted attacker.
    PassGuard's engine generates thousands of variants using your real information, then checks whether your password appears in that dictionary — simulating exactly what an attacker who knows you would do.
    The result is a personalized resistance score on top of the standard strength score. A password can score 75/100 on strength but still be in your personalized attack dictionary at rank #42.
